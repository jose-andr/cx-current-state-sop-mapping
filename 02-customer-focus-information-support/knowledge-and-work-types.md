# Knowledge and Work Types

## Function

Customer Focus Information Support

## Purpose

This file categorises the types of knowledge and work performed by the Customer Focus Information Support function.

The aim is to understand the shape of the work before drafting or validating individual SOPs.

## Current status

Status: Draft discovery placeholder.

Knowledge and work types should be validated with the officer who performs the function.

## Work type categories

| Category | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Recurring information maintenance | Regular updates to service information, customer service tools or knowledge sources. |
| Urgent information update | Time-sensitive update needed because of an emerging issue, service change, campaign or customer impact. |
| Ad hoc support request | Work triggered by a specific request from a staff member, editor, service area or manager. |
| Editor support | Guidance or coordination provided to people responsible for maintaining service information. |
| CRM or system support | Support related to CRM user issues, customer service tools or related applications. |
| Hot topic response | Work triggered by campaigns, media issues, public events, service disruption or topics likely to generate customer contact. |
| Content quality improvement | Work that improves clarity, usefulness, accuracy or first contact resolution value of service information. |
| Exception handling | Work that happens when information is missing, conflicting, outdated, unclear or disputed. |
| Informal workaround | Manual or unofficial work used to complete tasks where systems, ownership or process are unclear. |
| Shared responsibility | Work that depends on another team, editor, service area, communications team or system owner. |
| Role gap | Work expected by others or described in role documents but not currently performed. |
| Future capability signal | Work that may be needed in a future operating model but is not current practice. |

## Knowledge type categories

| Knowledge type | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Service rule | A rule that explains how a service works. | Eligibility, fee, timeframe or requirement. |
| Process guidance | Instructions for staff on how to handle a service enquiry. | What to ask, where to send, what to check. |
| Customer-facing explanation | Plain-language explanation used to support a customer response. | What the customer needs to do next. |
| Evidence requirement | Information or documents needed to complete a service interaction. | Proof of residence, permit number or account detail. |
| Escalation pathway | Guidance on when and where to escalate. | Contact service owner if issue is urgent. |
| Hot topic briefing | Time-sensitive guidance on an emerging issue. | Campaign, disruption, media issue or public concern. |
| System guidance | Instructions on how to use a tool or CRM function. | Where to update or find a record. |
| Editor guidance | Instructions for people maintaining service content. | How to update a Service Directory entry. |
| Status or outage information | Temporary service information. | Service unavailable, delay or known issue. |
