# Role Context

## Function

Customer Focus Information Support

## Current-state framing

This function supports accurate, current and usable service delivery information for customer-facing staff and related customer service systems.

The current-state mapping should focus on how the function operates today, including recurring information maintenance work, urgent updates, customer service tool updates, CRM user support and support for first contact resolution.

## Practical role focus

The function is currently understood to focus on:

- Service Directory content maintenance
- customer service information updates
- customer service tool maintenance
- CRM user issues and requests
- telephone messages on hold
- urgent or hot topic customer response information
- communication of new developments to branch staff
- support for Service Directory editors
- improvement of content that supports first contact resolution

## Role intent versus actual practice

The role description identifies a broad mix of routine and unplanned activities.

Each responsibility should be tested against actual current practice before it becomes a current-state SOP.

Some work may be:

- recurring and suitable for SOP mapping
- urgent or event-driven
- informal or undocumented
- shared with another officer, team or service area
- dependent on individual knowledge
- dependent on unclear ownership or approval pathways

## Why this function matters

Customer Focus Information Support affects the quality and consistency of customer service.

If service information is outdated, unclear, duplicated or difficult to find, staff may be less able to resolve enquiries at first point of contact.

This can create:

- inconsistent responses
- avoidable escalations
- repeat customer contact
- staff uncertainty
- duplicated information across tools
- delays during urgent or high-profile issues
- weak foundations for reusable omnichannel knowledge

## Current-state discovery questions

Use these questions during the officer mapping session:

1. What work lands on your desk in a normal week?
2. Which information updates happen regularly?
3. Which updates are urgent or reactive?
4. Who requests service information changes?
5. Who approves information changes?
6. What information sources do you rely on?
7. What systems or tools do you update?
8. Where is information duplicated?
9. What makes content good enough for first contact resolution?
10. What happens when information is unclear, outdated or conflicting?
11. What CRM user issues or requests come to you?
12. What hot topics usually require customer response coordination?
13. How are urgent updates communicated to branch staff?
14. What support do Service Directory editors need?
15. What work depends on your personal knowledge?
16. What work is shared with other teams?
17. What work appears in the PD but does not happen in practice?
18. What information should be reusable across channels in future?
