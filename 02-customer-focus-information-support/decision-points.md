# Decision Points

## Function

Customer Focus Information Support

## Purpose

This file captures the decisions made during Customer Focus Information Support work.

Decision points are important because they show where the officer applies service knowledge, content judgment, approval rules, stakeholder coordination or escalation criteria.

They also reveal what needs to be documented, governed or made reusable in future.

## Risks linked to decision points

| Risk | Related decision | Potential impact |
|---|---|---|
| Publishing inaccurate information | Is the requested information accurate and approved? | Customers and staff receive incorrect guidance. |
| Updating the wrong tool but not the source of truth | Which channel or tool should be updated? | Duplicated or inconsistent information. |
| Missing an urgent branch update | Does this need branch-wide communication? | Staff continue using outdated responses. |
| Treating a system issue as a content issue | Is this a CRM user issue or a system escalation? | Delay, misrouting or unresolved issue. |
| Leaving ownership unclear | Who owns the service information? | Content becomes outdated or disputed. |
| Poor first contact resolution content | Is the content good enough for first contact resolution? | More escalations, repeat contacts or staff uncertainty. |

## Omnichannel implications

| Deciding which tool to update | Future architecture should separate source knowledge from publishing channels. |
