# Exception Pathways

## Function

Customer Focus Information Support

## Purpose

This file captures exceptions that occur when Customer Focus Information Support work does not follow a standard path.

Exceptions are important because they reveal hidden complexity, unclear ownership, approval gaps, duplicated information, outdated content, urgent communication risks and future knowledge governance needs.

## Common exception types

| Exception type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Missing information | Required service information is absent. | No current guidance for a customer enquiry type. |
| Outdated information | Published information no longer reflects current service delivery. | Service timeframe has changed but the entry has not been updated. |
| Conflicting information | Different tools or teams provide different guidance. | Service Directory and intranet show different instructions. |
| Unclear ownership | No clear owner for approving or maintaining the information. | Update request cannot be confirmed. |
| Urgent issue | Information must be updated or communicated quickly. | Media issue or operational change creates customer contact. |
| System or access constraint | Tool prevents the required update. | Officer cannot publish or edit the relevant page. |
| Approval delay | Required approver is unavailable or unclear. | Content remains outdated while awaiting sign-off. |
| Scope ambiguity | It is unclear whether the request belongs to this function. | CRM user issue may be system support rather than information support. |
| Quality gap | Content exists but is not useful enough for staff. | Entry does not help staff answer the enquiry at first contact. |

## Workarounds

Use this section to capture manual or informal methods used to handle exceptions.

| Workaround | Used when | Why it exists | Risk | Future implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Emailing service area for clarification | Service information is unclear or missing. | No clear source of truth or approval pathway. | Slow response and poor traceability. | Future knowledge model needs clear ownership and approval. |
| Posting urgent update through Teams or email | Information needs to reach staff quickly. | Standard publishing path may be too slow. | Staff may miss or lose the update. | Future alerting workflow may be needed. |
| Manually copying content across tools | Same information appears in multiple places. | Tools are not connected. | Inconsistent updates and duplication. | Headless or reusable content model may reduce duplication. |
| Using personal knowledge to resolve request | Guidance is not documented. | Process or content standard is incomplete. | Single-person dependency. | Knowledge capture and SOP governance needed. |

## Omnichannel implications

Exceptions in this function are important for future omnichannel design because service knowledge needs to be accurate, reusable and governed across channels.

| Exception pattern | Omnichannel implication |
|---|---|
| Missing service information | Future channels may be unable to provide complete guidance. |
| Conflicting information across tools | Future source-of-truth rules are needed. |
| Manual copying between tools | Reusable or headless content architecture may be needed. |
| Urgent updates rely on informal channels | Future operating model needs structured rapid update workflows. |
| Content ownership is unclear | Service knowledge needs clear owners, maintainers and review cycles. |
| Content is not useful for first contact resolution | Future knowledge objects should be designed around staff and customer task completion. |
