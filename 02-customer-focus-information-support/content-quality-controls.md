# Content Quality Controls

## Function

Customer Focus Information Support

## Purpose

This file captures the checks, safeguards and rules used to maintain the quality of customer service information.

Content quality controls are important because service information affects staff confidence, first contact resolution, customer consistency, urgent response coordination and future reusable knowledge design.

## Content quality dimensions

Use these dimensions when mapping service information quality.

| Dimension | Meaning | Example question |
|---|---|---|
| Accuracy | Information reflects the current service rule, process or requirement. | Is this information correct? |
| Currency | Information is up to date. | Has anything changed since this was last reviewed? |
| Completeness | Information includes enough detail for staff to act. | Can staff answer the enquiry using this entry? |
| Clarity | Information is easy to understand and use. | Is the instruction clear? |
| Consistency | Information aligns across tools, teams and channels. | Does this match what is published elsewhere? |
| Ownership | A person, role or team is accountable for the information. | Who owns this content? |
| Approval | Updates are checked by the right person before publication. | Who has approved this change? |
| Findability | Staff can locate the information when needed. | Can staff find this quickly? |
| Usefulness | Information supports enquiry resolution or the next best action. | Does this help staff resolve the customer issue? |
| Reusability | Information can be reused across tools, channels or workflows. | Could this be a reusable knowledge object? |

## Possible control failures

| Failure | Related control | Potential impact |
|---|---|---|
| Information is published without source confirmation | Source confirmation | Staff may provide inaccurate advice. |
| Content is changed without approval | Approval check | Service owner may dispute guidance. |
| No owner is identified | Ownership check | Content may become outdated. |
| Review cycle is missing | Currency check | Old information remains in use. |
| Information differs across tools | Cross-tool consistency check | Staff and customers receive inconsistent guidance. |
| Content does not support first contact resolution | First contact resolution check | More escalations and repeat contact. |
| Urgent update is not flagged | Urgency check | Staff may not receive timely guidance. |
| Update is not confirmed as published | Publishing confirmation | Requested change may not be live. |
| Staff are not told about important change | Staff communication check | Customer-facing responses remain inconsistent. |

## Content quality questions

Ask these during discovery:

- What makes a Service Directory entry good?
- What information must be included?
- What information is optional?
- Who confirms accuracy?
- Who approves updates?
- Who owns the information after publication?
- How often is content reviewed?
- How do you know information is outdated?
- How do staff report incorrect information?
- How do editors know what standard to follow?
- How do you check whether content appears somewhere else?
- How do you know whether staff can use the content for first contact resolution?
- What content should be retired?
- What content should be reused across channels?
- What content should never be changed without approval?

## Omnichannel implications

| Content quality control | Omnichannel implication |
|---|---|
| Source confirmation | Future channels need trusted service knowledge sources. |
| Approval check | Reusable content needs clear approval before distribution. |
| Ownership check | Knowledge objects need named owners and maintainers. |
| Currency check | Future content model needs review cycles and expiry rules. |
| Cross-tool consistency check | Headless or reusable content architecture may reduce duplication. |
| First contact resolution check | Knowledge should be designed around customer and staff task completion. |
| Urgency check | Future model needs rapid update pathways for emerging issues. |
| Publishing confirmation | Future workflow needs visible publication status. |
| Staff communication check | Staff channels need structured update alerts. |
