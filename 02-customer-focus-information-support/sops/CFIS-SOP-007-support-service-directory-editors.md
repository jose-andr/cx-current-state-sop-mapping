## 1. Purpose

This SOP documents the current-state process for supporting Service Directory editors.

The purpose is to understand how editors are supported to keep service information accurate, current, useful and aligned to customer service needs.

This SOP should be validated with the officer who performs the work before it is treated as confirmed current practice.

## 2. Scope

## In scope

- supporting Service Directory editors and key staff
- responding to editor questions
- helping editors improve content quality
- coordinating communication with editors
- encouraging updates to outdated or unclear content
- identifying content quality issues
- escalating ownership, approval or access issues
- supporting consistent use of Service Directory standards

## Out of scope

- formal editor training program design
- enterprise knowledge governance design
- HR capability development
- system configuration
- access provisioning unless confirmed as current practice
- final future-state editor operating model

## 5. Current process

Draft process to validate:

1. Receive or identify a Service Directory editor support need.
2. Clarify what the editor or staff member needs help with.
3. Review the relevant Service Directory entry or content issue.
4. Determine whether the issue relates to:
   - content accuracy
   - content clarity
   - missing information
   - outdated information
   - ownership or approval
   - publishing process
   - system access or tool issue
5. Provide guidance or support where within scope.
6. Help identify the right source information, owner or approver if required.
7. Escalate issues that cannot be resolved directly.
8. Record any agreed changes, follow-up or unresolved issues according to current practice.
9. Confirm whether the editor can complete the update or whether further support is needed.

## 12. Omnichannel implications

Service Directory editor support reveals what governance and capability may be needed for future reusable service knowledge.

Current-state observations may inform future requirements such as:

- named knowledge owners and maintainers
- editor roles and responsibilities
- content quality standards
- content review cycles
- source confirmation rules
- approval pathways
- editor support model
- reusable guidance templates
- issue tracking for recurring content quality problems
