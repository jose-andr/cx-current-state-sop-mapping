## 1. Purpose

This SOP documents the current-state process for coordinating customer response information for hot topics.

A hot topic is an emerging issue, campaign, media topic, public event, service disruption or operational change likely to generate customer contact.

The purpose is to understand how hot topics are identified, how customer-facing response information is prepared, who is involved, how information is approved, and how updates are communicated to staff.

This SOP should be validated with the officer who performs the work before it is treated as confirmed current practice.

## 2. Scope

## In scope

- identifying hot topics likely to generate customer contact
- gathering source information
- coordinating with relevant teams
- preparing or confirming customer response guidance
- supporting Contact Centre or branch staff with current information
- updating relevant customer service tools where required
- communicating urgent or important updates
- retiring or updating guidance when the topic changes

## Out of scope

- media strategy
- public relations ownership
- campaign strategy
- emergency management governance
- formal incident management
- service redesign
- final future-state rapid response model

## 5. Current process

Draft process to validate:

1. Identify or receive notice of a hot topic likely to generate customer contact.
2. Clarify what the topic is and why it matters for customers.
3. Identify the responsible service area, communications contact or topic owner.
4. Gather source information and current key messages.
5. Assess whether customer-facing staff need guidance.
6. Prepare or coordinate response guidance for customer-facing staff.
7. Confirm accuracy and approval of the response guidance.
8. Update relevant customer service tools if required.
9. Communicate the guidance to relevant branch or Contact Centre staff.
10. Monitor whether the topic changes or further updates are required.
11. Update, retire or archive the guidance when the issue has passed.

## 12. Omnichannel implications

Hot topic response coordination is important for omnichannel readiness because emerging issues can affect multiple channels at once.

Current-state observations may inform future requirements such as:

- rapid update workflows
- topic ownership rules
- approved key message pathways
- staff alert mechanisms
- temporary content expiry rules
- cross-channel consistency checks
- reusable hot topic briefing templates
- escalation rules for conflicting information
- publication and retirement tracking
