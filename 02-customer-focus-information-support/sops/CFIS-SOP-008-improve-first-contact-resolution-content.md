## 1. Purpose

This SOP documents the current-state process for improving service information so customer-facing staff can resolve more enquiries at first point of contact.

The purpose is to understand how content gaps, unclear guidance, repeated questions and escalation patterns are identified and translated into improved customer service information.

This SOP should be validated with the officer who performs the work before it is treated as confirmed current practice.

## 2. Scope

## In scope

- identifying service information that does not support first contact resolution
- reviewing Service Directory or related content for clarity and usefulness
- gathering feedback from customer-facing staff
- identifying missing, unclear or incomplete guidance
- working with service owners or editors to improve entries
- updating or supporting updates to customer service information
- capturing recurring content improvement opportunities

## Out of scope

- full service redesign
- formal customer journey redesign
- broad channel strategy
- performance reporting unless confirmed as current practice
- chatbot or automation design
- final knowledge management framework design
- formal training program design

## 5. Current process

Draft process to validate:

1. Identify content that may not support first contact resolution.
2. Review the existing entry or guidance.
3. Clarify what staff need to know or do to resolve the enquiry.
4. Identify what is missing, unclear, outdated, too complex or not actionable.
5. Confirm the correct service information with the service owner, editor or approved source.
6. Draft or recommend improved content.
7. Check whether the improved content is accurate, clear, complete and usable.
8. Confirm approval or ownership where required.
9. Update the relevant customer service tool or support the editor to update it.
10. Notify relevant staff if the improvement changes how enquiries should be handled.
11. Record the improvement or unresolved issue according to current practice.

## 12. Omnichannel implications

First contact resolution content is a strong signal for future reusable service knowledge.

Current-state observations may inform future requirements such as:

- first contact resolution content quality standard
- reusable service knowledge object types
- staff task-based content design
- customer intent mapping
- next-best-action guidance
- escalation pathway standards
- content ownership and review cycles
- cross-channel consistency rules
- feedback loops from staff enquiries to knowledge improvement
