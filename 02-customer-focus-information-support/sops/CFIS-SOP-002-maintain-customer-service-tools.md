## 1. Purpose

This SOP documents the current-state process for maintaining customer service tools used to support customer-facing staff and service delivery information.

The purpose is to understand which tools are maintained, what information is updated, who requests or approves changes, and where duplicated information or unclear ownership may exist.

This SOP should be validated with the officer who performs the work before it is treated as confirmed current practice.

## 2. Scope

## In scope

- maintaining customer service information tools
- updating Customer Focus Portal, Customer Service Directory, CoMWeb, intranet or related tools where confirmed as current practice
- checking source information
- confirming ownership or approval
- publishing or updating content
- recording completed updates
- identifying duplicated or conflicting information across tools
- escalating unclear, conflicting or access-constrained updates

## Out of scope

- enterprise content management strategy
- website governance
- CRM system configuration
- technology platform administration
- chatbot or automation design
- final source-of-truth architecture
- approved future-state publishing workflow

## 5. Current process

Draft process to validate:

1. Receive or identify a customer service tool update need.
2. Confirm which tool or tools are affected.
3. Review the current content.
4. Check whether the requested change is clear and complete.
5. Confirm the source information and content owner where required.
6. Identify whether similar information appears in another tool.
7. Update the relevant tool or prepare the update for approval or publishing.
8. Check that the updated content is accurate, clear and usable.
9. Confirm whether customer-facing staff need to be informed.
10. Record the update according to current practice.
11. Escalate unclear, conflicting, urgent or access-constrained updates.

## 12. Omnichannel implications

Customer service tools are part of the current knowledge ecosystem.

Current-state observations may inform future requirements such as:

- source-of-truth rules
- tool purpose and audience definitions
- reusable service knowledge objects
- cross-tool consistency checks
- content ownership and approval pathways
- publication status tracking
- retirement rules for outdated content
- headless or reusable information architecture
