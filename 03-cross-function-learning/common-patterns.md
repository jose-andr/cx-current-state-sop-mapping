# Common Patterns

## Purpose

This file captures common patterns identified across the current-state SOP mapping for:

- Customer Data and Systems Support
- Customer Focus Information Support

The intent is not to merge the functions too early.

The intent is to identify shared conditions, dependencies and future operating model signals after each function has been mapped separately.

## Emerging shared themes

## 1. Quality assurance is happening manually

Both functions appear to protect service quality through manual review, checking, correction and follow-up.

This is valuable current-state work, but it may also indicate that upstream controls are weak or not consistently embedded.

Future design implication:

- define prevention controls
- define detection controls
- define correction controls
- identify where manual judgement must remain
- identify where workflow, rules or automation could assist

## 2. Governance gaps show up as operational work

When ownership, approval, source-of-truth or escalation pathways are unclear, the work becomes slower and more dependent on individual relationships.

Future design implication:

- name data owners and knowledge owners
- define approval pathways
- define escalation thresholds
- define review cycles
- document decision rights

## 3. Systems are connected through people, not architecture

The current state may rely on officers knowing where information lives, who to ask and how to resolve inconsistencies.

Future design implication:

- map system dependencies
- identify duplicated information
- define authoritative sources
- document handoffs
- reduce hidden coordination effort

## 4. Current-state issues are future business case evidence

Pain points should not only be treated as operational annoyances.

They may become evidence for a future business case around:

- data quality management
- service knowledge management
- content governance
- CRM improvement
- customer identity consistency
- headless or reusable knowledge
- omnichannel service readiness
- staff productivity
- first contact resolution
- risk reduction

## 5. Staff confidence depends on trust in data and knowledge

Customer-facing staff need to trust both the customer record and the service guidance they use.

Future design implication:

- define what trustworthy information means
- improve visibility of update status and source
- make escalation pathways clear
- design staff-facing guidance around task completion
- reduce conflicting or outdated information

## Important boundary

Common patterns do not mean the two functions should be merged.

Current-state mapping should preserve the difference between:

- customer data quality and record integrity
- service information quality and knowledge usability

Shared patterns should be used to identify governance, system and operating model implications only after actual practice is understood.
