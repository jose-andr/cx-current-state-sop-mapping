## 1. Purpose

This SOP documents the current-state process for recording progress of customer data cleansing, correction and de-duplication work.

The purpose is to understand how completed, pending, escalated and unresolved work is tracked today.

This SOP should be validated with the officer who performs the work before it is treated as confirmed current practice.

## 2. Scope

## In scope

- recording completed data cleansing work
- tracking pending or unresolved customer data issues
- recording escalated cases
- noting work that could not be completed
- maintaining visibility of progress
- supporting continuity if work needs to be resumed or reviewed

## Out of scope

- formal reporting and analysis of CX performance
- organisation-wide KPI reporting
- formal audit framework design
- system configuration changes
- enterprise records management decisions

## 5. Current process

Draft process to validate:

1. Complete or pause the relevant data cleansing, correction or de-duplication activity.
2. Identify the current status of the work:
   - complete
   - pending
   - escalated
   - unresolved
   - not a duplicate
   - requires further review
3. Record the customer record reference using the current approved method.
4. Record what action was completed or why the work could not be completed.
5. Record any escalation, handoff or follow-up required.
6. Update the current tracker, system note, queue or worklist.
7. Confirm whether any further action is required.
8. Return to the next record, task or assigned work item.

## 12. Omnichannel implications

Progress tracking matters for omnichannel readiness because unresolved customer data issues can affect customer context across channels.

Current-state observations may inform future requirements such as:

- visible data quality work queues
- standard status categories
- unresolved issue tracking
- audit trail expectations
- escalation ownership
- privacy-safe progress notes
- integration between data quality tasks and CRM records
