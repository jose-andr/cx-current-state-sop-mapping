## 1. Purpose

This SOP documents the current-state process for cleansing and simplifying customer records.

The purpose is to understand how customer records are reviewed, corrected and improved so they are more accurate, complete, current and usable.

This SOP should be validated with the officer who performs the work before it is treated as confirmed current practice.

## 2. Scope

## In scope

- reviewing customer records for quality issues
- identifying inaccurate, incomplete, outdated or inconsistent data
- correcting customer data where permitted
- simplifying records where duplicate, redundant or confusing information exists
- recording cleansing actions
- escalating unclear or high-risk cases

## Out of scope

- final approved data governance standards
- customer experience measurement and reporting
- system configuration changes
- large-scale data migration
- automation rule design
- customer-facing communication unless directly required by the process

## 5. Current process

Draft process to validate:

1. Open the customer record requiring review.
2. Check the record for obvious data quality issues.
3. Review relevant fields for accuracy, completeness, currency, uniqueness and consistency.
4. Compare information against available source records or related systems where required.
5. Identify what information should be corrected, retained, removed, simplified or escalated.
6. Confirm whether the officer has enough information and authority to make the change.
7. Apply permitted corrections in the relevant system.
8. Record the change or progress according to current practice.
9. Escalate unclear, sensitive, conflicting or system-constrained cases.
10. Mark the record or task as complete, pending or unresolved using the current tracking method.

- duplicate prevention at intake
- customer context carry-over across channels
