# Risk Register Template

## Purpose

Use this template to capture risks discovered during current-state SOP mapping.

Risks should describe what could go wrong in the current state and what impact that could have on customers, staff, systems, data, service knowledge, governance or future omnichannel readiness.

## Instructions

Capture each risk in plain language.

Separate the risk from the cause, impact and current control.

Mark assumptions clearly.

## Risk categories

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Customer experience | Risk may affect customer accuracy, consistency, timeliness or trust. |
| Staff confidence | Risk may affect staff ability to answer, resolve or act confidently. |
| Data quality | Risk may involve incorrect, duplicate, incomplete or unreliable customer data. |
| Knowledge quality | Risk may involve outdated, unclear, duplicated or incomplete service information. |
| Governance | Risk may involve unclear ownership, approval, standards or decision rights. |
| Workflow visibility | Risk may involve hidden work, unclear status, missed follow-up or poor auditability. |
| Continuity | Risk may involve reliance on one person, memory or informal relationships. |
| System dependency | Risk may involve tool access, system limitations or duplicated systems. |
| Omnichannel readiness | Risk may block future reuse, automation or cross-channel consistency. |

## Likelihood guide

| Likelihood | Description |
|---|---|
| High | Expected to occur often or already occurs repeatedly. |
| Medium | Occurs sometimes or under known conditions. |
| Low | Uncommon but possible. |
| Unknown | Not enough evidence yet. |

## Impact guide

| Impact | Description |
|---|---|
| High | Could create customer harm, incorrect advice, major rework, compliance issue or major service inconsistency. |
| Medium | Could create staff friction, delay, duplicated effort, avoidable escalation or moderate inconsistency. |
| Low | Could create minor inconvenience with limited service impact. |
| Unknown | Not enough evidence yet. |

## Current control prompts

- What prevents this risk today?
- What detects the risk if it occurs?
- What corrects the risk after it occurs?
- Is the control documented?
- Does the control depend on one person?
- Is evidence retained?
- Is the control reliable?
- What happens if the control fails?

## Mitigation prompts

- Could ownership reduce the risk?
- Could a clearer rule reduce the risk?
- Could a checklist reduce the risk?
- Could a system prompt reduce the risk?
- Could workflow visibility reduce the risk?
- Could a review or expiry date reduce the risk?
- Could staff guidance reduce the risk?
- Could better source-of-truth rules reduce the risk?
