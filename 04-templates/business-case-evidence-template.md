# Business Case Evidence Template

## Purpose

Use this template to capture current-state evidence that may support a future business case.

The purpose is not to write the business case yet.

The purpose is to collect reliable evidence about effort, risk, rework, delay, duplication, staff impact, customer impact and future omnichannel constraints.

## Instructions

Only capture evidence that can be linked to current-state practice.

Separate evidence from interpretation.

Mark uncertain evidence clearly.

Do not include sensitive customer information.

## Evidence categories

| Category | Description | Example evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Volume | How much work occurs. | Number of duplicate records reviewed, content updates requested or urgent updates sent. |
| Effort | Time or labour required. | Time spent checking, correcting, chasing approval or updating multiple tools. |
| Rework | Work repeated because the first attempt did not resolve the issue. | Duplicate corrections, repeated staff questions or repeated content fixes. |
| Delay | Waiting time or process bottlenecks. | Waiting for source confirmation, approval, access or system support. |
| Risk | Current-state exposure to service, governance, data or knowledge quality risk. | Incorrect records, outdated guidance or unclear ownership. |
| Duplication | Same data, information or work maintained in multiple places. | Service information copied across tools or duplicate customer records. |
| Manual workaround | Work done manually because systems or governance do not support the task. | Spreadsheets, email chasing, manual checks or informal handoffs. |
| Staff impact | Effect on staff confidence, workload or ability to resolve enquiries. | Staff uncertainty, avoidable escalation or difficulty finding guidance. |
| Customer impact | Effect on customer experience. | Repeat contact, inconsistent advice, delayed response or incorrect information. |
| Omnichannel blocker | Current-state condition that would block future channel reuse or automation. | Untrusted data, unowned content, weak review cycles or unclear source of truth. |

## Quantitative evidence prompts

Use these prompts where safe and practical.

- How many items are handled per day, week or month?
- How many duplicate records are reviewed?
- How many records are corrected?
- How many content updates are requested?
- How many urgent updates are sent?
- How many messages on hold are active?
- How many Service Directory entries need review?
- How often are staff questions repeated?
- How often are requests misrouted?
- How long does the work usually take?
- How long is spent waiting for approval or clarification?
- How much rework happens?
- How many systems or tools need to be checked?

## Qualitative evidence prompts

Use these prompts where numbers are not yet available.

- What makes the work difficult?
- What creates uncertainty?
- What creates staff frustration?
- What creates customer inconsistency?
- What depends on memory?
- What depends on informal relationships?
- What breaks when the usual person is unavailable?
- What would happen if the work stopped?
- What would be risky to automate without governance?
- What current-state issue would block omnichannel service?

## Evidence quality guide

| Evidence strength | Description |
|---|---|
| Strong | Direct example, validated by person performing the work, or supported by reliable operational data. |
| Medium | Supported by examples or repeated stakeholder feedback but not yet quantified or fully validated. |
| Weak | Based on assumption, memory, role description wording or one unvalidated example. |

## Safe handling rules

- Do not store customer personal information.
- Do not include screenshots unless redacted and approved.
- Do not include sensitive staff comments without permission.
- Prefer aggregate counts over individual cases.
- Use de-identified examples.
- Mark evidence as unvalidated until reviewed.
- Do not use aspirational role scope as business case evidence unless clearly labelled as future intent, not current-state practice.
