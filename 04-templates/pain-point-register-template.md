# Pain Point Register Template

## Purpose

Use this template to capture pain points discovered during current-state SOP mapping.

Pain points should describe friction in actual work today.

They should not jump straight to solutions.

## Instructions

Capture each pain point in plain language.

Use evidence where possible.

Mark assumptions clearly.

## Categorisation

Use these categories to group pain points during synthesis.

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Ownership | Unclear owner, approver or accountable decision-maker. |
| Process | Steps, handoffs, timing or workflow are unclear or inefficient. |
| System | Tool limitations, access issues, duplicated systems or poor fit. |
| Data quality | Incorrect, duplicate, incomplete or unreliable customer data. |
| Knowledge quality | Outdated, unclear, duplicated or incomplete service information. |
| Governance | Missing standards, unclear decision rights or weak controls. |
| Visibility | Work status, progress, review dates or approvals are hard to see. |
| Continuity | Work depends on one person, memory or informal relationships. |
| Customer impact | Pain point may affect customer service, accuracy or consistency. |
| Staff impact | Pain point may affect staff confidence, effort or first contact resolution. |

## Severity guide

| Severity | Description |
|---|---|
| High | Creates customer impact, significant risk, repeated rework or major delay. |
| Medium | Creates staff friction, moderate delay, manual workaround or inconsistent handling. |
| Low | Creates inconvenience but limited service or risk impact. |
| Unknown | Needs further evidence. |

## Evidence prompts

- What example shows this pain point?
- How often does it happen?
- Who notices it?
- Who is affected by it?
- What workaround is used?
- What happens if the workaround fails?
- What evidence can be safely captured without exposing customer information?
- Does this pain point appear in more than one SOP?
- Does this pain point support a future business case?
